Colorado Wolf & Wildlife Center

Responsive Website Redesign

Project Details

Website Redesign
Create a Responsive Web Design, with an improved user experience, hopefully leading to increased Online Donations.
Timeline
June 2022
Goal
Conduct user research, user interviews. Restructure the information architecture. Establish a modern design concept. After several rounds of iteration & user testing, deliver a High-fidelity Responsive Web Design.
My Role & Responsibilities
User Interviews, Generate Style Guide, Mid & High Fidelity Mockups, User testing, Prototyping

User Research

We began our process with 5 user interviews to better understand users motivations when choosing to donate to a non-profit as well their personal reasons for visiting the CWWC website. We learned users are more likely to donate money when they can trust the organization and when they feel a connection to the cause.

We also observed each of those users interacting with the current site. Practically every user described the current site as “Medieval” mostly due to the banner typography. Mostly users did not respond positively to the current hero photo of the wolf eyes, letting us know how important approachable photos of the wolves would be. They negatively responded to the “distracting buttons,” thought the color choices were weird and every user commented on how dense and daunting the text on each page was.

Problem Statement

TThe Colorado Wolves & Wildlife Center website was designed to spread awareness of wolves. We observed that their website is not clear on what their objective and mission is, which is causing low online user retention to their business. How might we improve the website so that their users are successful based on increase in donations?

Testing Insights

From testing a prototype of our low-fidelity mockups we confirmed that we were on the right track with our new layout and navigation system. Users seemed to intuitively understand how to navigate the site. We did discover that we should further simplify some of the information architecture especially when it came to the listing of tours. Although our overall goal was to increase donation rates, we learned from users that they wanted to learn more about the CWWC before seeing a request to Donate button.

Testing Insights

There were a few more items in our information architecture that we realized users were initially searching for under a different category. Users seemed to love all the photos of wolves that we integrated throughout the site, but said they wanted even more photos. We discovered a few alignment issues and realized we should add arrows to Hero Header so users would have the freedom to click through the animated slideshow faster and wouldn’t have to wait a whole rotation to return to an image/”call to action.”
